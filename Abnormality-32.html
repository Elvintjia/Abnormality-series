Abnormality-32

Alias: Pick-lock paperclip

Danger level: 0

  1. Description
  2. Test logs
    • Test 32-11

Description

Abnormality-32 is a paperclip measuring roughly 5 cm and made of iron. Abnormality-32 can be used to open any kind of doors or locks, without the need to be skilled or experienced at picking locks. No known lock or security system can prevent the use of Abnormality-32. Even vaults without keyholes or state of the art security systems cannot resist Abnormality-32. Abnormality-32 can only be used by each person once to open a lock or door. Any attempts to use Abnormality-32 by the same person will always result in a failure. Abnormality-32 is resistant to acid and rust.

Test logs

Test 32-1

Experiment: Abnormality-32 was used to open a door locked from the opposite side.

Result: The door was unlocked.

Test 32-2

Experiment: Abnormality-32 was used to unlock a hotel door.

Result: The door was unlocked. The door registered the insertion of a keycard.

Test 32-3

Experiment: Abnormality-32 was used to open a car door.

Result: The car became unlocked.

Test 32-4

Experiment: A safe without a keyhole was attempted to be opened with Abnormality-32.

Result: The safe was opened with ease. Simply pressing Abnormality-32 against the safe was enough to open it.

Test 32-5

Experiment: A bank vault with biometric system security was attempted to be opened with Abnormality-32.

Result: Simply sticking Abnormality-32 on the biometric system was enough to bypass the electronic security system.

Test 32-6

Experiment: A car engine was attempted to be turned on with Abnormality-32.

Result: Abnormality-32 can be inserted into the slot of the car key, but the entity could not activate the car engine.

Test 32-7

Experiment: A combination lock was attempted to be unlocked with Abnormality-32.

Result: Abnormality-32 was slipped on one of the lock’s opening and the lock was immediately unlocked.

Test 32-8

Experiment: An online account was attempted to be hacked with Abnormality-32.

Result: No matter how it was attempted, it failed.

Additional notes: What were you expecting?

Test 32-9

Experiment: An automated metal door with no keyhole was attempted to be opened with Abnormality-32.

Result: The metal door opened upon contact with Abnormality-32. After an experimental subject entered through the door, the door closed.

Test 32-10

Experiment: A submarine with nuclear weapon was attempted to be entered with Abnormality-32.

Result: The hatch could be opened the moment Abnormality-32 touched it.

Test 32-11

Experiment: Abnormality-32 was inserted into a nuclear weapon activation keyhole.

Result: Abnormality-32 could be inserted but could not activate the nuclear weapon.

Additional notes: No nuclear weapon was involved during this test. Only the nuclear weapon operating system was used.

Test 32-12

Experiment: A password electronic lock was attempted to be bypassed with Abnormality-32.

Result: Sticking Abnormality-32 to the electronic lock immediately unlocked the door.

Test 32-13

Experiment: An unlocked door was attempted to be locked with Abnormality-32.

Result: The door was successfully locked.

Test 32-14

Experiment: A door was attempted to be unlocked by Abnormality-32 with the same experimental subject as the one in test 32-13.

Result: The door could be unlocked.

Test 32-15

Experiment: An experimental subject was ordered to unlock a door with Abnormality-32, and then ordered to lock the door with Abnormality-32.

Result: The door was unlocked and locked.

Test 32-16

Experiment: An experimental subject was ordered to lock two doors one after another with Abnormality-32.

Result: Subject could lock the first door, but the second door couldn’t be locked.

Test 32-17

Experiment: An unlocked biometric door was locked with Abnormality-32.

Result: The door could not be opened by anyone except the same experimental subject with Abnormality-32. Other experimental subject with Abnormality-32 could not open it.

Test 32-18

Experiment: A door without locks was attempted to be locked with Abnormality-32.

Result: The door could not be locked.

Addenda

Addendum 32-1

Test results have shown that Abnormality-32 can open anything, including vehicle doors, safes, and any lock in this world, regardless of how sophisticated or complex. Even doors without keyholes or biometric locks can be opened with Abnormality-32. Exceptions are digital locks such as account password, phone pin password, and the likes. Abnormality-32 can only be used as a lock. Any attempt to use it otherwise, for instance, as a key car, will not work.

Test 32-2

It has been revealed that Abnormality-32 can also be used to lock doors. It has been determined that every person can each lock doors and open doors with Abnormality-32 just once. There appears to be no limit to which door can be locked with Abnormality-32. Exceptions are doors with no locks. Biometric doors can be locked with Abnormality-32, but it will result in the door being impossible to be reopened except with Abnormality-32, and by the same person. Investigation is underway to determine how far Abnormality-32’s locking and unlocking capability goes.

Origin

Abnormality-32 was found between the belongings of a thief. The thief had managed to break in a bank and steal a large sum of money. Security was tight and no malfunctioning or hacking was detected within the bank’s security system. However, the thief had left fingerprints and DNA during his infiltration. The thief’s face was also recorded on camera. A large scale operation was conducted to find the thief and the stolen money. The thief was caught and arrested a week after the heist. When interrogated, the thief claimed to have a miracle paperclip that can be used to open any lock. Police dismissed the thief’s claim. Watchmen caught wind of the incident. After investigation, it was found that the thief’s claim is correct. Abnormality-32 was later handed to the organization.
