Danger Level Classification

Every abnormality is assigned a danger level. Danger levels are assigned based on how dangerous and how likely it is to cause death. Danger levels are only meant to give a rough estimation of how dangerous an abnormal object is. Do not use it as a standard of how to treat or approach an abnormality.

Danger level 0: Cannot cause death or harm at all

Danger level 1: Can cause death or harm if handled improperly

Danger level 2: Actively pose as a danger (Abnormalities that constantly harm people around them are also categorized as this)

Danger level 3: Can cause breakout incidents

Danger level 4: Difficult to contain or can cause widespread damage

Danger level 5: Threat to humanity
