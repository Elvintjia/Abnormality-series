Description

Abnormality-39 is a small penguin doll measuring 30 cm in height, 15 cm in length and width, and 1 kilogram in weight. Abnormality-39 is sentient and alive. Abnormality-39 is capable of interacting with human beings. However, Abnormality-39 only moves in the absence of humans above the age of 11. Abnormality-39 will move towards children under the age of 11 and seek one when there is none in the vicinity. Abnormality-39 can move at a maximum speed of 0.5 m/s and is acrobatic enough to climb furniture. Abnormality-39 can traverse vents as well as hide itself as necessary when humans above the age of 11 is in the general vicinity of the entity. Abnormality-39 can be observed when moving by camera or other recording devices. Abnormality-39 can also be observed while moving as long as the entity is unaware of being observed, for example, with a one way mirror. Abnormality-39 has no method of causing harm directly, and avoids doing anything that may cause harm to humans. When Abnormality-39 interacts with a human child, the human child will always be surprised and fascinated by the entity. The child will always like Abnormality-39, and will never tell anyone else regarding the entity’s living nature. If Abnormality-39 is taken away from a child, the child will miss Abnormality-39 for a period of several days, before forgetting and no longer remembering about Abnormality-39. As far as observation goes, no harmful effects have ever been recorded of Abnormality-39’s interactions with a human child. As such, testing the entity with children is permitted. Abnormality-39 must be kept under watch at all times, as the entity will actively seek a way out of the facility and get to a child below the age of 11. The containment room must be secured to prevent Abnormality-39’s escape. Abnormality-39 seem to require no sustenance, and has no way of communicating with humans other than using hand signals.

Test logs

Test 39-1

Experiment: Abnormality-39 was locked inside a cage. Escape attempts by Abnormality-39 was observed.

Result: Abnormality-39 escaped the cage by opening the lock and forcing it open with a coin the entity produced out of nowhere.

Test 39-2

Experiment: A remote controlled robot was made to approach Abnormality-39.

Result: Abnormality-39 was confused and avoided interaction with the robot. Whenever approached, Abnormality-39 would run away from the robot.

Test 39-3

Experiment: Abnormality-39 was dropped from great heights.

Result: Abnormality-39 did not seem to suffer any repercussions.

Additional notes: Once again, do not use subjected to fall damage in the documents. use dropped from great heights.

Test 39-4

Experiment: A picture of a child was put in Abnormality-39’s containment room.

Result: Abnormality-39 repeatedly tried to hug the child in the picture, yet failed miserably.

Additional notes: The entity may not be as sentient as originally thought to be.

Test 39-5

Experiment: Water was poured on Abnormality-39.

Result: Abnormality-39 became wet. No other effect was recorded on the entity.

Test 39-6

Experiment: Abnormality-39 was put in a taped cardboard box.

Result: Abnormality-39 managed to escape by making a small hole on the side of the box and enlarging it until the entity could get out.

Test 39-7

Experiment: The voice of a child was played repeatedly on a speaker in Abnormality-39’s containment room.

Result: Abnormality-39 kept trying to embrace the speaker.

Test 39-8

Experiment: A crying baby was put in Abnormality-39’s containment room.

Result: Abnormality-39 comforted the baby. The baby stopped crying soon after.

Test 39-9

Experiment: Abnormality-39 was put inside a nailed wooden box.

Result: Abnormality-39 cut a hole open with a butter knife the entity produced.

Additional notes: How the hell can a butter knife cut open a hole in a wooden box?

Test 39-10

Experiment: Abnormality-39 was put inside a cabinet.

Result: Abnormality-39 escaped the cabinet by dismantling the hinges using a screwdriver. Camera feed shows Abnormality-39 producing the screwdriver out of nowhere.

Test 39-11

Experiment: Abnormality-39 was locked inside a safe.

Result: Abnormality-39 somehow got the safe to open. Camera feed inside showed the entity simply tried to open the safe and it opened.

Test 39-12

Experiment: A screen with a replayed video of a child crying was put in Abnormality-39’s containment room.

Result: Abnormality-39 tried to hug the monitor repeatedly.

Test 39-13

Experiment: A child with a phobia of penguins was put inside Abnormality-39’s containment room.

Result: Abnormality-39 embraced the child despite the child’s fears. Abnormality-39 interacted with the child continuously for the next 30 minutes. When the child was brought out of Abnormality-39’s containment room, the child was no longer afraid of penguins.

Test 39-14

Experiment: Dirt was smeared on Abnormality-39.

Result: Abnormality-39 cleaned itself. No tools were ever given to Abnormality-39. Abnormality-39 became clean and as good as new.

Test 39-15

Experiment: Abnormality-39 was put inside a bathtub and the bathtub was filled to the brim with water.

Result: Abnormality-39 sunk in the water. Abnormality-39 is incapable of getting out by itself.

Test 39-16

Experiment: A doll of a baby was put inside Abnormality-39’s containment room.

Result: Abnormality-39 tried to interact with the baby for 30 minutes before giving up or realizing the baby was fake.

Test 39-17

Experiment: A doll of a penguin was put inside Abnormality-39’s containment room.

Result: Abnormality-39 didn’t even interact with the doll.

Test 39-18

Experiment: A jack in the box was put inside Abnormality-39’s containment room.

Result: Abnormality-39 showed signs of fear when the jack in the box made a noise and came out of the box. Abnormality-39 approached it and was no longer scared after roughly 5 minutes.

Test 39-19

Experiment: A picture of a baby and a voice recording of a baby’s sound was put inside Abnormality-39’s containment room. The two objects are put 3 meters apart.

Result: Abnormality-39 walked back and forth between the two objects with seeming confusion.

Test 39-20

Experiment: A cat was put inside Abnormality-39’s containment room.

Result: Abnormality-39 was afraid of the cat and ran from it whenever the cat approaches the entity.

Addenda

Addendum 39-1

An easier method of containment has been found. It is found that it is easier to simply have a staff member or a sentinel carry Abnormality-39 around to prevent Abnormality-39’s escape as Abnormality-39 is only capable of movement when no adults are present. Abnormality-39’s old containment room is to be used only for Abnormality-39’s testings. The personnel carrying Abnormality-39 is to be changed daily.

Additional notes: We should have thought about this containment method earlier.

Addendum 39-2

Abnormality-39 seems to have the ability to escape any imprisonment. Any attempts to lock Abnormality-39 in a kind of container will always result in Abnormality-39 escaping. Abnormality-39 has the intelligence to try and look for an escape route whenever the entity is trapped. Whatever tools Abnormality-39 needs for its escape will be produced by unknown means. Additionally, it has been determined Abnormality-39 is not as smart as previously thought. Abnormality-39 is unable to differentiate between a picture of a child and a real child. Recorded noises of a child can also fool Abnormality-39. Water and strong force seems to have no effect on the entity.

Addendum 39-3

Abnormality-39 has been determined to be inherently fearful of moving objects or living things that are non-human. Animals and moving objects such as jack in the box or robots will terrify Abnormality-39. Abnormality-39 will typically run and hide, never resorting to violence. Additionally, Abnormality-39 has been shown to be able to discern dolls apart. Abnormality-39 will always ignore dolls and rarely interact with them. However, baby dolls can fool Abnormality-39 for approximately 30 minutes before Abnormality-39 will begin ignoring it, unlike recording and pictures that can fool Abnormality-39 for an indefinite amount of time. Abnormality-39 has also been shown to be capable of cleaning itself and incapable of drowning.

Origin

Abnormality-39 was found within a store. During closing hours, Abnormality-39 had wandered around a store and left the store to search for a child. CCTV footage confirmed the entity’s presence. Abnormality-39 was soon tracked and confiscated. Abnormality-39 had been put along with other penguin dolls of similar designs. The dolls had only been shipped to the store the day before and put on the store rack. The truck driver bringing in the toys were not aware of any oddities within the shipment. The staff at the toy factory was likewise unaware too. However, it has been determined that including Abnormality-39 in the total number of dolls shipped would make the number equal to the planned shipment. It is unknown if one of the dolls were switched with Abnormality-39, or the doll came to life and became Abnormality-39.
